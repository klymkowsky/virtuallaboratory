Cell Theory - Knowledge Statements

  • Organisms are composed of cells and their by-products.
  • All cells arise from pre-existing cells.
  • Evidence supports the premise that all modern organisms (and their cells) are derived from a common ancestral cell.
  • Eukaryotic cells appear to be the result of a unique endosymbiotic event with an aerobic eubacteria (α-proteobacterium).
  • Plant cells appear to have underwent a subsequent unique endosymbiotic event with a photosynthetic eubacteria (cyanobacterium).
  
  • Multicellular organisms are commonly composed of two generic cell types; germ line cells that can give rise to the next generation of organism, and somatic cells, that form the body of the organism.
  • Mutations in germ line cells can influence subsequent generations (and evolution), mutations in somatic cells do not.
  • Germ line cells are potentially immortal, somatic cells have a finite lifespan.
  • An organism can be composed of a single cell, multiple cells of the same type, or multiple types of cells, produced by the process of differentiation, which involves differential gene expression.

Cell Theory - Questions to ponder

  • What conditions, both environmental and selective, might drive the initial formation of a eukaryotic cell?
  • What obstacles would have to be overcome along the evolutionary pathway from prokaryotic to eukaryotic cell organization?
  • What factors would drive the mitochondrial → mitochondrial + chloroplast sequence of eukaryotic evolution?
  • What factors would select against the chloroplast → mitochondrial + chloroplast sequence?
  • What evolutionary advantages can you imagine are associated with the germ line-soma distinction? What evidence would you use to test your hypotheses?
  • What factors drove the evolution of various forms of multicellularity and sociality?
  • Why multicellularity is not universal?

Cell Theory - Performance Expectations

  • Make a claim, using data and evidence, to support the single common cellular ancestor hypothesis.
  • Diagram the steps in the appearance of the first cell and the evolution of the eukaryotic cell; justify each step in terms of underlying physiochemical principles and selective advantages.
  • Diagram the steps to various forms of multicellular biological organization (community, organism, society) indicating the factors involved in driving that event, as well as the evidence that justifies your sequence.
