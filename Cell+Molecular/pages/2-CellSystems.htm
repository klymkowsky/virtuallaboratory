Cells as non-equilibrium systems - Knowledge statements

  • Cells are adaptive, homeostatic, non-equilibrium, open (dissipative) systems; they obey the laws of thermodynamics.
  • Cells rely on systems of coupled reactions to drive thermodynamically unfavorable, synthetic processes.
  • These processes are catalyzed primarily by protein-based catalysts (enzymes).
  • Enzyme activity can be regulated through various mechanisms, these include where within the cell the protein is, whether it is catalytically active, and whether it is stable.
 
  • The ultimate source of the energy that keeps cells alive comes from the import and breakdown of unstable chemicals and/or the capture of light, which is used to generate new unstable chemicals (e.g. lipids, carbohydrates, proteins, nucleic acids, etc.)

Questions to ponder & performance expectation

  • How can you tell if two reactions are coupled?
  • Why is reaction coupling an essential element of biological systems?
  • How would you distinguish a steady state from an equilibrium system?
Questions to ponder & performance expectation
  • Develop a model of a coupled reaction system; generate a graph of its various components over time and how they respond to various perturbations (e.g. changes in the concentration of one component of the system).
  • Diagram a chemical reaction (your choice); describe how changes in system temperature influence the rate of the reaction at equilibrium.
  • Diagram a non-equilibrium system at steady state; explain the inputs and outputs of the system and the physical laws that dictate its behavior.
  • In the context of your system, indicate where and why would catalysts would be necessary.
