Systems Biology for Biologists is designed introduce biology students (with only passing familiarity with mathematics) to the principles that apply to, and shape, the interactions between molecules, genes, cells, tissues and organisms and lead to a range (perhaps all) biological behaviors.

We will consider molecular, cellular and organismic interaction networks in various contexts from the microbial and social to the pathological.

While the course could stand alone, it might be best taken after you have completed MATH 1310: CSM: Calclulus, Systems & Modeling, as well as the core MCDB courses.

We will use beSocratic activities and the Note Bene social reading groups to help you apply, test, and consolidate your learning. Class time will be spent working with the most difficult ideas in greater detail.

 Systems Biology for Biologists is a part of an attempt to build a "coherent curriculum" that includes biology, chemistry, and maybe even physics (someday.)
