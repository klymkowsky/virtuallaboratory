Networks: their roles in biological systems
download pdf

At the heart of all living systems are networks of interactions.  At the molecular level, these involve energy capture and molecular transformation systems.  The most of obvious of these are the systems of chemical reactions involved in metabolism.

A second type of molecular network, again common to all forms of life, involves genes and their products. This laboratory will focus primarily on the behavior of these genetic systems, but the behaviors we will discover, various regulatory interactions, direct, cooperative, feed-forward, feed back interactions, together with behaviors, such as steady state, switch-like, oscillatory, and hysteresis, will be found again and again in "higher order" networks. 

What are some of these networks?  They include networks formed by the interactions between cells within a body and between organisms.   Your own nervous and immune systems, not to mention the various process of embryonic development that formed you, are examples of network interactions. 

Similarly, the interactions between organisms, predator-prey, etc., can also be seen as an ecological network.
