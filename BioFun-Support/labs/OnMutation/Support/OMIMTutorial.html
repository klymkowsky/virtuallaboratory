Searching OMIM - some simple rules.

Searching the OMIM is very much like searching any other data base. What you get is what you look for. The more specific your request, the more likely you will find exactly what you want. Get too specific, and you may not find anything.

When you search OMIM you can retrieve genes and their descriptions or specific genetic diseases.

Take our current example, chaperones and human disease. If you search using just "chaperone" you will get back oa list of every gene that has been implicated as a chaperone, as well as genes whose products are known to interact with chaperones. You can make your search more specific by adding a word.

For example, searching for "disease chaperone" your list is greatly reduced. But if you searched for "disease chaperone groEL" you would get nothing (or at least I did when I tried it).

When you see a chaperone-based disease that interests you, you may want to explore it further by following the literature reference links.
