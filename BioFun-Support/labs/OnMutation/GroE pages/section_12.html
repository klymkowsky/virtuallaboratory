Gro E, heat shock & cell survival

GroE is essential for the survival of E. coli at all temperatures, so that only conditional or partial "loss of function" mutations in these genes can be isolated.

We can see the effects of GroE expression by a simple experiment.

We will briefly "heat shock" a culture of bacterial cells (10^6 CFU/ml) by bringing them to 45°C for 5 minutes. This is not enough to kill them, but is enough to induce the increased synthesis of GroE.

Return the heat shocked cells to 37°C for 10 minutes. Now compare the viability of control and heat shocked cells at 50°C.
