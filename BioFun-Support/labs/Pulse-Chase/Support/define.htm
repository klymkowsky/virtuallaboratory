Beer's Law Part 1

Look carefully at the dial of the virtual spectrophotometer.

There are two scales: "Absorbance" (A) and "Percent Transmittance" (which we will refer to as %T).

%T means exactly what you might think; the percentage of the light that makes it through the sample. %T is related to A by the simple formula:

A = -log(%T).

A %T reading of 90% (0.90) corresponds to an Absorbance of about 0.046 (try it with a calculator).

Beer's law can be viewed as the linear relationship between %T and the dry biomass of our bacterial sample.

A simple thought experiment should convince you of the validity of our use of Beer's Law.
Imagine, you are seated looking toward a light source, such as a television. Now imagine that a cloud of smoke floats in between you and the light. Smoke is just tiny particles suspended in the air and this cloud allows only 50% of the light from the source to reach where you are sitting (%T=0.5). "Fine", you say, "half the light that is coming from the light source gets through to me."

Now imagine that a second, identical cloud floats in between you and the first cloud.
