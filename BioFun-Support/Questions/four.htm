How did Redi rule out that the maggots that appeared on rotting meat were formed spontaneously?

He sterilized the meat
He blocked access of the meat to adult flies
 He presented a compelling argument that spontaneous generation was impossible

Why did the invention of the microscope rejuvenate the discussion of spontaneous generation?


It seemed possible that 'simpler' microscopic organisms could arise spontaneously
It was possible to actually see smaller organisms appear spontaneously
 Many more organisms were visable than previously

Spallazzani did not prove that

Boiling could sterilize a broth
The appearance of new microorganisms correlated with exposure to the air
 Microorganisms were present in air

Why is the apparent absence of spontaneous generation in the modern world important?


It implies a divine creation of life
It implies that modern organisms are descended from previous organisms
It implies that evolution, at least on the early earth, was impossible

Why were Redi's experiments important?


They suggested that macroscopic organisms could not arise spontaneously
They disproved the possibility of spontaneous generation for all organisms
They suggested that smaller organisms were generated by spontaneous generation.

If spontaneous generation were completely and totally impossible, that would imply


a supernatural creation / creator at least to create the first living thing.
the continual intervention of a supernatural being.
that evolution was impossible
