Major concepts in the biological sciences

under construction:

  • Physiochemical foundations of life - biological systems are completely compatible with, and based upon physical and chemical principles.  There are no "vital" or supernatural forces involved.
    • living systems do not violate the laws of thermodynamics
    • the ordered nature of living systems comes at the cost of increased entropy in the universe as a whole
  • The cell theory: all organisms are composed of cells or substances synthesized by cells; cells arise exclusively from other cells.
  • The theories of evolution: natural and sexual selection, genetic drift and other "sampling" effects are involved in the change in populations over time.  Splitting of populations gives rise the the diversity of organisms.
