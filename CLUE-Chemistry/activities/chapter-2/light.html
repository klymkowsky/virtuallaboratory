Thinking about light: 

Light is weird, it acts like a particle and a wave, depending upon how you look at it.  Nothing is your experience behaves this way [you can watch Richard Feynmann lecture about this, if you like].

Scientifically, light is composed of quantum mechanical particles, known as a photons.  The intensity of light is determined by the number of photons passing by per second.

The energy of a particular photon is given by the equation E = constant x frequency.  
 
The frequency of a wave is x is related to the wavelength (Î») = c/frequency, were c is the speed of the wave.
