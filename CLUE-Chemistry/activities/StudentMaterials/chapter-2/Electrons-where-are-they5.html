It is likely that we need more data, is every type of atom different is a random way, or are their trends we can spot. Here is were the fact that each element differs from the others by some integer number of protons (and electrons). We can therefore arrange all types of atoms in a linear manner, that is, in terms of increasing numbers of protons/electrons.

We can then ask, is there an understandable tread in ionization energies as proton/electron number increase?

Here are the ionization energies for the elements Na (11 protons/electrons), Mg (12), Aluminum (13), Silicon (14), Phosphorus (15), Sulfur (16), Chlorine (17), and Argon (19).

The ionization energy (generally) gets larger – indicating that the size of the atom is (generally) getting smaller. We can explain this observation by saying that the outermost electron of each element (the one that is being removed) is located in a different quantum shell.
