Avogadro's assumptions

Avogadro assumed that, at the same temperature and pressure, equal volumes of gases contain equal numbers of particles (molecules) and that the relative densities of gases reflected the relative weights of the these particles.

Let us consider the implication of these assumptions (together with Dalton's atomic assumptions.)
