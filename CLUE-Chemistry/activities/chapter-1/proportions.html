The law of constant proportions
If, as Dalton assumed, all molecules are made up of (essentially) indestructible atoms and if all molecules of the same type have the same number and types of atoms, then it should be possible to answer these questions.

Suppose you have a sample of a material and it contains C to H atoms in a 1:2.5 ratio, what is simplest possible formula for the molecule (and why)?

  

We are given two samples of what are supposed to be the same pure substance. Upon analysis, we find that substance 1 has a ratio of 1 carbon to 3 hydrogen to 1 oxygen atom, while substance two has a ratio of 1 carbon to 2 hydrogen to 2 oxygen atoms. What are we to conclude (and why)?

You discover a compound with a ratio of 0.5 C to 2 hydrogen atoms. Given Dalton's assumptions, what would be the simplest molecular formula (and why)?
