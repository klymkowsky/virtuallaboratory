Internet resources
  We use a number of internet-based resources, both in biological research and teaching this course.

You may want to down-load some programs and familiarize yourself with some of the more useful sites. These include

xrefer   An search engine restricted to dictionaries and encyclopedias. Generally produces the answers to questions more efficiently than google.
PubMed   This is the entrance to the entire world of biological science literature. It is also the gateway to various powerful tools to search sequence and structural data. Well worth learning how to use this site.
OMIM   The on-line mendelian inheritance in man database contains almost all there is to know about the genetic and molecular basis of disease in humans.
Cn3D   This a very cool 'helper' application that will enable you to visualize and play with real structures deposited in the NCBI data base. Can be quite useful in really understanding structural questions.
NCBI
Bookshelf		   A number of publishers have made access to their textbook available through the NCBI site. Can be useful when you are looking up a topic or an alternative explanation, and it beats spending serious money on a book.
Talk-Origins   A very useful site about all issues evolutionary.
